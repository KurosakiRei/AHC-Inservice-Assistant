1.What is the best definition of affirming care in home health?
Delivering respectful, inclusive, and individualized care to every patient

Delayed or avoided health care

Politely ask, "What pronouns do you use?"

Discrimination based on sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression

Lesson 2 of 10
Fire Safety in Home Care Settings
Watch 3 videos
1. According to the RACE method, what should you do first?
Rescue anyone in immediate danger.

2. What are the top three most important aspects of fire safety in a home care environment?
•	Following the RACE method during a fire emergency
•	Ensuring smoke detectors are functional and emergency exits are clear
•	Identifying common fire hazards and addressing them proactively

3. Why does oxygen increase the risk of fire?
It makes materials ignite and burn faster.

4. What is the safest action to take when the fire alarm sounds and you are caring for a client who uses a wheelchair?
Assist the client using their wheelchair and follow the planned evacuation route.

Hand Hygiene and Infection Control
Watch 1 video
1.When should you wash your hands to effectively prevent the spread of germs in a home care setting?
Before and after direct contact with clients, and after removing gloves

2.What is the primary reason for washing hands before and after direct contact with clients in a home care setting?
To prevent the spread of harmful germs and protect both clients and staff

3.What is the most effective method for removing germs from visibly dirty hands?
Handwashing with soap and water
